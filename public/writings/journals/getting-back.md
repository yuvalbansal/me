It has been a long time since I wrote my heart out. So here we go.

## Looking back

First of all, I would like to begin with my history of writing. I used to write regularly when I was in my early teens. In fact, I did have a blog page where I used to write all of my thoughts and journals. I looked for ideas that fascinated me and jotted them down on my notes app. Every evening, I let those thoughts flow into a journal entry. Occasionally, I wrote poems as well, which were never posted to the blog. My purpose for having the blog was to record my experiences in an easy-to-read and universally accessible format without losing anonymity. While I would admit that I have never been very good at it, I loved writing as it gave me an opportunity to organise my thoughts and express without judgement. But all of this is a story of the past.

I am not 14 anymore, I am 21. I am not in high school, I have just completed my undergrad and I am about to start working professionally in about six weeks. I want to clarify that this is not a sudden urge to write. Whenever I cross any milestone in my life, material or philosophical, I get an itch to write. Instead of giving in to it, I turned to other people.

## Transition period of my life

As I retrospect today, I realise that I have been too occupied in all of the time I spent in college. I do not regret it at all. If anything, I am grateful for it, because those years allowed me to grow exponentially.

The unambitious leisure that I have today has come up with strange challenges for me. I struggle to save myself from falling into the trap of excessive forethought. I did have my targets formulated, alarms set and schedule prepared at the beginning. But eventually, it all fell apart. And for the first time in years, instead of pushing myself, I allowed it to derail. Instead of trading my time for certainty and planning the fallback for the fallback, to which I was accustomed to, I let it unfold itself.

## Reluctance to writing

I have had this longing to write from over the past one month, as soon as I completed my undergrad. Yet, instead of courageously throw myself into it, I started to worry about the inspiration and platform for my writings, subjects which were never of great concern to me.

As for the latter, I built a personal website that would store my writings. At the moment, the website contains everything except what it was built for. I classified my writings as poems, journals and articles on the initial version of my website. As I was inspecting my way of writing, I noticed that it doesn't typically fit into categories like journals or articles. I have always started writing with a description of my day and the immediate affairs of my life, eventually moving on to more specific, predecided or discovered, ideas that would strike my interest. Nonetheless, I will stick to the initial classification and term the articles less technically as ideas on my webpage.

## Rediscovery

When I started to write, words were slipping from my mind, leaving me defenceless against my thoughts as they came too honestly and fiercely while I was unable to write them. Contrary to what I had anticipated, writing aimlessly did not bring back my old habit of uninhibit writing. In past few years, the ideas I came across, the people I met and my experiences have taught me so much that it would require innumerable journals for me to write it down.

Years have gone by and I feel disparate to the version of myself whose methods kept me captivated to writing. I have grown more aware, thoughtful and somewhat timid about expressing with conviction. However, I believe that timidness will improve the quality of my writings, making my thoughts less susceptible to wandering. I would hope that I get back, spend a good amount of time introspecting and refine my beliefs in the rest of my days at home.
